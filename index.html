Remembering Mother Teresa: A Life of Compassion and Service

  • Founding the Missionaries of Charity:

    In 1950, Mother Teresa founded the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic congregation dedicated to serving the poor, sick, orphaned, and dying. The organization's primary focus was on providing care and dignity to those in need.

  • Work in Calcutta, India:

    Mother Teresa's work in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India, gained international recognition. She and her sisters opened hospices, homes for abandoned children, clinics, and centers for the mentally ill.

  • Commitment to the Poorest of the Poor:

    Mother Teresa's unwavering commitment to the most marginalized and impoverished people earned her the nickname "Saint of the Gutters." She believed in seeing the face of Jesus in each person she served.

  • Global Outreach:

    The Missionaries of Charity expanded beyond India, establishing centers worldwide, including in Africa, Europe, and the Americas. The organization continues to provide humanitarian aid today.

  • Nobel Peace Prize:

    In 1979, Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize for her tireless efforts to alleviate suffering and promote peace. She donated the prize money to the poor.

  • Philosophy of Love and Compassion:

    Mother Teresa emphasized the importance of showing love and compassion to all, regardless of their background or circumstances. Her simple acts of kindness inspired many.

  • Canonization as a Saint:

    Mother Teresa was beatified by Pope John Paul II in 2003 and canonized as a saint by Pope Francis in 2016. Her canonization recognized her exemplary life of selflessness and service.

  • Legacy of Inspiration:

    Mother Teresa's legacy continues to inspire individuals and organizations worldwide to engage in charitable work and make a positive impact on the lives of the less fortunate.

  • Missionaries of Charity Today:

    The Missionaries of Charity continue to serve the poor, sick, and marginalized across the globe. Their work includes providing medical care, education, and support to those in need.
